My Uber Nightmare

I feel like I am a victim of a loophole that Uber has overlooked, one that protects the rider more than the victim (the rider) in this case, by not punishing a driver who cancels on a trip in the middle of a 2-stop ride without any notice or warning to the rider. And if you so happened to leave anything in the driver's car (because why carry all that baggage with you, when you know that the driver is obligated to take you to your second stop), your stuff now becomes mis-labeled as "lost items" because you "forgot to take them with you" when your trip concluded.

Well, no Uber, my trip DIDN'T conclude because I was still owed another stop. And what's even more fucked up is that the Uber receipt shows the driver successfully completing her "1" stop trip with me, not showing any record of my original request of 2-stops and the driver secretly cancelling it behind my back. Now, the driver told me "the program cancelled by itself", but that's bullshit, I've done PLENTY of 2-stop rides before with other drivers, and that has never happened. I've also asked other SEASONED Uber drivers, and they said that the app doesn't just execute commands whenever it wants; it requires a user interacting with it to tell it what to do.

So I tried to contact the driver MANY times via the Uber app; she only picked up the phone on my initial call to her, when I asked her why she ditched me, and if she could pick me up again because she had my vape in her backseat. I tried to request a ride with HER as the driver to no avail. After a couple of hours had passed, I tried to contact her again via the Uber app but this time she was done trying to help me. The Uber employees, who are acting as the middlemen between me and her, they tell me she doesn't have it. Ok, so WHAT HAPPENED TO IT? THAT'S NOT AN ACCEPTABLE CLOSING STATEMENT THAT GIVES ME CLOSURE ON THE ISSUE.

Any attempts on my part to follow-up using the Uber app to get answers now go unanswered by the Uber employees themselves, who are I guess tired of me trying to get my $100 vape back. So for you Uber drivers out there, next time you get a rider with baggage requesting a 2-stop ride, try to convince them to leave their baggage in the car while you wait for them at their first stop, then when they aren't looking, drive away with their belongings and make sure to cancel the trip after successfully dropping them off at their first stop. Uber won't think you stole anything, in fact, they will consider that to be the riders' fault for losing their items in your car.

Lastly, if you are ever in the Orange County, Southern California area, beware of a Middle Eastern woman with first name Samaneh, driving a blue 2021 Toyota Camry with license plate #8VRA633 (she's the bitch that screwed me over).
